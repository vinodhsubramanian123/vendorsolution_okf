# 01. Vision & Business Problem

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the purpose, business vision and engineering objectives of the Infrastructure Knowledge Platform (IKP).

It explains **why IKP exists**, **what engineering problems it solves**, and **what Antigravity is expected to build**.

This document intentionally focuses on the business and engineering domain rather than implementation technologies.

---

# 2. Vision

Infrastructure engineering knowledge is currently fragmented across thousands of independent sources including technical documents, vendor portals, APIs, engineering spreadsheets, firmware repositories, customer BOQs and human expertise.

IKP exists to transform this fragmented information into a continuously evolving Engineering Knowledge Platform capable of understanding engineering data, reasoning over customer requirements, validating solutions and continuously improving through learning.

IKP is not a document management system.

IKP is not a chatbot.

IKP is an Engineering Knowledge Platform.

---

# 3. Business Problem

Infrastructure engineers spend significant time locating, interpreting and validating information before recommending a solution.

Typical activities include:

* Reading hundreds of PDF pages
* Searching multiple vendor portals
* Comparing generations
* Understanding compatibility
* Identifying dependencies
* Understanding firmware requirements
* Validating configurations
* Correcting portal errors
* Building multiple solution options
* Comparing cost vs performance
* Explaining recommendations

The knowledge exists.

The problem is that the knowledge is fragmented.

---

# 4. Current Challenges

Engineering information is distributed across independent systems.

Examples include:

* Vendor Product Documentation
* QuickSpecs
* User Guides
* Installation Guides
* Release Notes
* Compatibility Matrices
* Firmware Catalogs
* Vendor Partner Portals
* Configuration Portals
* Validation Portals
* Customer BOQs
* Excel Sheets
* APIs
* Internal Engineering Notes
* Human Experience

Each source contains only part of the engineering knowledge.

No source independently provides a complete solution.

---

# 5. Vision of IKP

IKP SHALL become the central engineering knowledge platform responsible for:

* Understanding engineering information
* Preserving engineering knowledge
* Building canonical engineering models
* Supporting engineering reasoning
* Validating engineering recommendations
* Learning from engineering feedback
* Continuously improving engineering intelligence

The implementation SHALL allow engineers to focus on engineering decisions rather than information discovery.

---

# 6. Engineering Philosophy

IKP does not store documents.

IKP understands engineering knowledge contained within documents.

Documents remain evidence.

Knowledge becomes the primary organizational asset.

Engineering knowledge SHALL remain:

* Structured
* Explainable
* Searchable
* Reusable
* Traceable
* Continuously maintainable

---

# 7. Business Capabilities

The completed platform SHALL provide the following business capabilities.

## Engineering Knowledge Acquisition

Acquire engineering knowledge from heterogeneous engineering sources without requiring manual organization.

---

## Engineering Knowledge Understanding

Understand engineering meaning rather than textual content.

Identify products, components, capabilities, relationships, rules, dependencies and engineering constraints.

---

## Engineering Knowledge Organization

Generate canonical engineering knowledge independent of source format.

Knowledge SHALL remain vendor agnostic wherever possible.

---

## Engineering Reasoning

Reason over customer requirements rather than searching documents.

Support engineering decisions using knowledge, relationships, constraints and evidence.

---

## Engineering Validation

Validate generated solutions using vendor validation systems whenever available.

Learn from validation outcomes.

---

## Engineering Learning

Continuously improve engineering knowledge using:

* Updated documentation
* Vendor portal feedback
* Human approvals
* Operational observations
* Customer implementations
* Knowledge deltas

---

## Engineering Recommendations

Generate multiple engineering solutions optimized for different customer priorities.

Examples include:

* Lowest Cost
* Balanced Configuration
* Maximum Performance
* AI Optimized
* Capacity Optimized
* Low Latency
* High Availability
* Future Growth

Every recommendation SHALL include engineering reasoning and supporting evidence.

---

# 8. Customer Journey

A typical engineering request follows this lifecycle.

```text
Customer Requirement

↓

Understand Customer Intent

↓

Identify Engineering Requirements

↓

Discover Relevant Engineering Knowledge

↓

Evaluate Compatible Products

↓

Evaluate Rules

↓

Evaluate Dependencies

↓

Evaluate Constraints

↓

Generate Candidate Solutions

↓

Validate Solutions

↓

Improve Solutions

↓

Present Alternatives

↓

Capture Learning

↓

Update Engineering Knowledge
```

IKP SHALL automate this lifecycle.

---

# 9. Success Criteria

IKP is considered successful when engineers no longer need to manually search multiple independent engineering sources to build and validate infrastructure solutions.

Instead, they interact with engineering knowledge.

The platform continuously:

* acquires knowledge
* improves knowledge
* validates knowledge
* reasons over knowledge
* explains recommendations
* learns from experience

while preserving engineering evidence, traceability and history.

---

# 10. Guiding Principle

> **Architecture is stable. Knowledge evolves. Intelligence improves.**

This principle governs every implementation decision within IKP.

The architecture should rarely change.

Engineering knowledge should continuously evolve.

Engineering intelligence should improve through evidence, validation and learning.
